Settlement

Create a home base and places for you to discover on your adventures. Roll up establishments, news, and NPCs to bring the settlement to life.

How Settlements Work

When you arrive at a new settlement (or create your starting town), roll 1d6 to determine how many establishments it has. Each establishment is a place your characters can visit — a tavern, a temple, a market stall, a guild hall.

Use the generator below to build settlements quickly. It rolls the establishments, generates names, and gives you settlement news — rumors and events that can seed quests or flavor.

You can also name your settlement using the Name Generator in the sidebar.

Settlement Tips

  • Revisit your settlement between adventures. Roll new Settlement News each time you return — the town evolves while you're away.
  • Keep a list of NPCs you meet. The NPC Dialogue tool in the sidebar helps when you need a conversation.
  • Not every establishment needs to be visited. Some exist as background — a blacksmith you might need later, a temple you haven't entered yet.
  • When you're ready to adventure, head to the Quest page to find your next objective.
