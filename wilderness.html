How Wilderness Exploration Works

Wilderness exploration has two phases: Early and Late. You start in the Early phase and transition to Late after you've traveled far enough (or when the generator tells you).

The Exploration Loop

Each step through the wilderness, the generator rolls for what you encounter:

  • Travel — uneventful progress toward your destination
  • Feature — a point of interest (landmark, ruin, natural wonder)
  • Challenge — a skill test or obstacle to overcome
  • Encounter — monsters or NPCs to interact with
  • Settlement — a town or camp along the way
  • Dungeon Found — you've arrived at your quest destination

Key Rules

  • Doubles: Rolling doubles on the exploration dice triggers a special event and grants 1 Luck point.
  • Weather: Changes periodically and can affect travel. Narrate it for atmosphere.
  • Monster Encounters: When the generator calls for monsters, use the Monster Encounter tools in the sidebar.
  • Treasure: Some encounters and features yield treasure — use the Treasure tool in the sidebar.

Wilderness Exploration Flow

Begin Journey
Roll Exploration Dice (2d6)
Check Result: Travel / Feature / Challenge / Encounter / Settlement / Dungeon
Resolve the Result
Play out encounters, narrate features, attempt challenges
Check for Phase Transition
Early → Late based on progress
Continue or Arrive at Dungeon
Loop until Dungeon Found
