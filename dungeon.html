How Dungeon Exploration Works

Like the wilderness, dungeon exploration has Early and Late phases. You explore rooms one at a time until you find and complete your quest objective.

Room Types

  • Empty Room — nothing immediately dangerous, but might contain clues or atmosphere
  • Feature Room — a notable dungeon feature (altar, fountain, collapsed section, etc.)
  • Challenge Room — a skill test, puzzle, or trap to overcome
  • Monster Room — creatures to fight or negotiate with
  • Treasure Room — loot! Use the Treasure tool in the sidebar
  • Objective Room — your quest target is here. Complete it to finish the dungeon.

Passages and Doors

  • After each room, the generator determines how many exits you find — passages, doors, or dead ends.
  • Locked doors require a key (found elsewhere in the dungeon) or a skill check to force open.
  • Trapped doors can hurt you if you don't detect and disarm them first.

Key Rules

  • Doubles: Same as wilderness — special event + 1 Luck point.
  • Lingering: If you spend too long in an area you've already explored, you risk a lingering encounter.
  • Backtracking: Returning through cleared rooms is safe, but lingering encounter rules still apply.
  • Monster Encounters: Use the Monster Encounter tools in the sidebar when the generator calls for combat.
  • Objective: Completing the objective in the Late phase means you can leave the dungeon and return to your settlement.

Loop until Objective Complete
